Wax Time Unauthorized Reissues
By
mattmattmatt
on April 24, 2014
Format: Vinyl
|
Verified Purchase
I bought this and a few others of these Wax Time reissues which the interwebs claim are sourced from CDs and NOT authorized from original 50s masters. I'm a professional musician but an amateur audiophile and was kind of disappointed that these were reproduced in Spain from CDs of public domain recordings not actual masters. These reissues are widely available for around $10-13 so caveat emptor. I'm less concerned that the extended estates and publishing interests of the original artists won't be paid for these recordings--AFAIK many of them weren't compensated fairly while they were alive. But from the perspective of someone who wants to own something "legit," it does bother me that these Wax Time reissues are broadly viewed as a joke.
Having said all that, I'm actually very happy with the quality of these bootlegs. The vinyl is heavy and flat. I've bought three and none were warped and all had nice sleeves, jackets and artwork. These aren't garbage records, but they're not at all the audiophile quality they claim to be. If you want to enjoy these recordings on a turntable, these are fine. But buyers should look into the whole story on these Wax Time reissues before buying.

Ella & Louis is Absolutely Wonderful
By
Inspired Dribble
on July 4, 2013
Format: Vinyl
|
Verified Purchase
The song selection and recording quality of the "Ella & Louis" vinyl LP is far far above most LP's out there today and is one of the best sounding LPs that I have heard (specifically, I am talking about the LP labelled import, pressed by 'Waxtime').  The recording is warm and filled with feeling and has a rare dimensional depth and soundstage with a "live -  in your living room" quality and presence, - and the selection of songs shows the extraordinary warmth and dynamic that the two have together.  It is easily one of the best recorded and mastered LPs I have heard.
The Ella & Louis LP is an absolute must have for anyone who likes Jazz, Ella or Louis, or truely great music in general.  The music spans generations and will appeal to all lovers of great song and artistry.
If you were wondering if you should get this LP, don't hesitate.  Just buy it.  It is destined to be a classic and I recommend that you get it before people realize just how great the LP is and it becomes in short supply.
By the way, this Ella & Louis LP (the one where the cover shows Louis with 'wader' pants pulled up by sitting in a chair and 1950's white socks showing, Waxtime label) is much better sounding and better mastered than the double vinyl LP "Together - Ella & Louis" (with a blue toned cover) which, although not bad, sounds a little lean and digital, and is lacking depth in comparison to  the better sounding single LP  "Ella & Louis" reviewed here.
